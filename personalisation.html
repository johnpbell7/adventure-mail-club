Personalisation

Your child is the story.

Not a name dropped in once. A character the whole group depends on.

How it works

Woven in. Not bolted on.

Most personalised letters just drop a name into a template. Adventure Mail Club is different. Your child's details, interests and replies are genuinely built into the story structure — not added afterwards.

1

Name & city

Not just “Dear [Name]” — their city appears on maps, in coordinates, in directions the team is heading. They are a location in the story.

2

Their interests

If they love dogs, a familiar bark appears. If they love drawing, they are asked to sketch something important. Their world enters the adventure.

3

Their own replies

Whatever they write or draw comes back in the next letter. Referenced. Used. Celebrated. Their words become part of the ongoing story permanently.

To: Amelia, Birmingham

Dear Amelia,

We have been thinking about the bridge near Birmingham you mentioned. Penny checked the map. It is on it — right at the edge of page three.

The golden dog with the torn ear you described. Nova saw it at the hollow entrance. It was waiting for you.

Your answer — south — was right. What we found is almost impossible to describe.

— The Wandering Five, Field Camp
